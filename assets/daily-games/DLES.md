# DLES a.k.a Daily Logic Exercises

An open mirror of the **881** daily games collected at [dles.gg](https://dles.gg/games/newest?ref=assets.dles.gg) ordered by newest first, updated weekly. Made a daily game yourself? [Submit it to the collection!](https://dles.gg/submit-game?ref=assets.dles.gg).

- [Border guesser](https://borderguesser.com?ref=assets.dles.gg) - Hold a button to progressively reveal a mystery country's border, with its continent given as a hint. Guess the country as early as you can: the less of the outline shown when you answer correctly, the higher your score. Work through 7 countries to finish.
- [Beatdle](https://beatdle.fun?ref=assets.dles.gg) - Listen to a drum beat, then recreate its rhythm on a 16-step sequencer across 3 tracks — kick, snare, and hi-hat. You can replay the target only once per guess, so memorize it. After each submission, every cell is marked correct, misplaced, or absent, Wordle-style, until you reproduce the full pattern within 6 attempts.
- [BallotGuessr](https://ballotguessr.com/daily?ref=assets.dles.gg) - You get dropped into a Google Street View somewhere in the U.S. and read the surroundings — architecture, businesses, signs, landscape — for political clues. Drag a slider between a strong Democratic and a strong Republican lean to predict how that county voted in the 2024 presidential election. The closer your guess, the more points you bank across 5 timed rounds.
- [Prince Chazz](https://princechazz.com?ref=assets.dles.gg) - You control a crowned piece that moves like a chess queen, any distance in a straight line or diagonal. Land on an enemy to capture it for a point, but each capture spawns 2 more, and every enemy moves like a chess piece (rook, knight, bishop) that hunts you after your turn. Rocks block your path, and a single enemy landing on you ends the run. Plan your moves and chain captures to push your score as high as you can.
- [Last Control](https://game.last-control.com/play?ref=assets.dles.gg) - Answer 9 rapid multiple-choice brain-teasers against a 60-second clock, spanning mental arithmetic, pattern completion, short-term memory recall, cipher decoding, and flag/trivia recall. Each correct answer raises your score out of 100, and you finish with a streak and a shareable result grid, all framed as a dystopian "cognitive audit".
- [Ballot Blocks](https://ballotblocks.com?ref=assets.dles.gg) - Look around a U.S. neighborhood in Google Street View and use a slider to guess who won and by how much in the 2024 presidential election — pick Harris or Trump and the margin. Set a wager (1x, 2x, or 3x) before answering — correct guesses earn points multiplied by your wager, wrong ones lose half. A bonus round follows each correct guess: place the birth or death year on a timeline for extra points.

Tap a cell to cycle through empty, grass (a "not here" marker), and mushroom. 3 sizes to choose from: Easy 8×8, Medium 10×10, and Hard 12×12, each a separate timed puzzle.
